Lake Turkana

The 7 million-hectare Lake Turkana, in Kenya's Northern Frontier, is the largest desert lake in the world. The lake's length exceeds 290 kilometers, its depth is 30 meters, and its widest point is 32 kilometers. With only a little portion of the northern tip stretching into Ethiopia, the majority of the lake is entirely contained within Kenyan territory. While Lake Turkana is famous for its enormous size, our visitors are also fascinated by the water's unusual greenish-blue tint, known as "jade," which is brought on by the presence of algae. More than 40 different fish species have been identified to exist beneath the surface of the Jade Sea, but the most recognizable predators are the Nile crocodiles, which sun themselves along the coastlines. Migratory birds can stop at Sibiloi National Park, the South and North Islands, and the Central Island of the lake, which has a small active volcano . You might get to interact with people from the Rendille, Gabra, Samburu, Turkana, El Molo, Ariaal, and Dassanech tribes that rely on Lake Turkana and the surrounding nature to survive.Each of these native peoples has adapted to the challenging surroundings by learning essential skills like deep lake fishing, gathering wild fruit and honey, and herding animals and camels. Lake Turkana and the national parks that surround it were chosen as a UNESCO World Heritage Site due to the variety of flora and wildlife, ecological importance of the region, and the relevance of archaeological and paleoanthropological finds on a global scale.  Contact us for a personalized itinerary and detailed guide.
